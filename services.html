Is daycare right for your dog?

Day care is not for every dog, and we want to ensure your dogs enjoy their time with us and s/he goes home to you happy. We provide a free meet and greet assessment: Not every dog is suited to day care and this time will allow us and your dog to see if we are a good fit. A time will be arranged with you to assess your dogs temperment and suitability to attend day care


Once accepted, what happens next?

Please book your dog in with us, if possible start with a half day. New dogs are introduced carefully and patiently, ensuring they do not get overwhelmed or stressed. At the assessment and again on their first few days, dogs are given an opportunity to sniff and explore for a few minutes. If your dog is feeling comfotable and tails are wagging, positive introductions are made. We want them to feel our day care is another home, and we are their 2nd family. We will work with you to continually assess that day care is the best place for your dog, but it is very important that you share any concerns with us. Please let staff know how your dog is reacting after they go home from day care.


What we ask of you?

Dogs need to be fully up to date on their vaccinations, including distemper and parvo. Copies of up to date vaccinations must be provided. Regular Parasite and flea treatments must be in place. We ask that you commit to a regular time/day, to ensure your dog has a routine annd we can plan for all our canine family members. Booking must be made in advance, and we have a 24hour cancellation policy. Payment is each friday Dogs over 6 months must be spayed/neuteured. Vet details to be provided.

More about us!

Regular Cleaning Routines. All staff are trained in canine first aid and cpr. Quiet Times. We start the day with socialisation time. Lots of equipment is provided for physical and mental agility. Lots of time is spent with your dog.
