Is daycare right for your dog?

Day care is not for every dog, and we want to ensure your dogs enjoy their time with us and they go home to you happy.
We provide a free meet and greet assessment as not every dog is suited to day care and this time will allow us and your dog to see if we are a good fit.
A time will be arranged with you to assess your dog’s temperament and suitability to attend day care.

Contact us

Once accepted, what happens next?

Please book your dog in with us, if possible, start with a half day.

New dogs are introduced carefully and patiently, ensuring they do not get overwhelmed or stressed.

At the assessment and again on their first few days, dogs are given an opportunity to sniff and explore for a few minutes.
If your dog is feeling comfortable and tails are wagging, positive introductions are made.
We will work with you to continually assess that day care is the best place for your dog, but it is very important that you share any concerns with us.
Please let staff know how your dog is reacting after they go home from day care.

Your dogs day

The day is structured between supervised playtime, mental stimulation games and quiet times.
We have lots of space, tunnels and fun interactive agility games, with separate play areas for our puppies and older dogs.
Rest is equally important as play, and we have a dedicated quiet time for all dogs. Rest areas are available when dogs needs some time away from the group.
Clean water freely available, with regular cleaning of all areas.

About Us

Before a team member spends any time with our canine family, they must complete a rigorous training course, designed by our animal behavioural and body language specialist. The maximum ratio between monitoring staff and the number of dogs is 1:6. Most importantly, all our team work hard to ensure each dog is having a positive experience.

What we ask of you?

  • Dogs must be fully up to date on their vaccinations, including distemper and parvo. Copies of up-to-date vaccinations must be provided.
  • Regular Parasite and flea treatments must be in place.
  • We ask that you commit to a regular time/day, to ensure your dog has a routine and we can plan for all our canine guests.
  • Booking must be made in advance, and we have a 48 hour cancellation policy.
  • Payment is each Friday.
  • Dogs over 6 months must be spayed/neutered.
  • Vet details to be provided.

What body signals mean:
  • Calm – ears and tail are relaxed
  • Alert – ears and tail are up
  • Fear – crouched with tail between legs
  • Aggressive – lips are pulled back and hackles, tail and rump are up
  • Calm – ears and tail are relaxed
  • Greeting – licking face, begging food, play bowing
